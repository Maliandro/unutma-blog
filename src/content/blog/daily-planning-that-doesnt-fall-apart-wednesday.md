Most “daily planning” fails for one boring reason: it assumes **stable energy**.

Monday’s plan is written by **rested you**. Wednesday is run by **human you**.

## The problem: your plan is a fantasy budget

If your day plan needs:

- Perfect focus blocks,
- Zero interruptions,
- Zero emotional drag,

…it is not a plan. It is a **wish list** with time stamps.

## Build plans that survive Wednesday

1. **Under-schedule deep work** — leave slack for messages, meals, and being alive.
2. **One anchor outcome** — “If nothing else happens, I finish X.”
3. **Repair loops** — a five-minute evening reset: what slipped, what moves tomorrow.

Planning is not about predicting the future. It is about **recovering faster** when the future is rude.

## Bottom line

Daily planning that lasts is **Wednesday-proof**: smaller promises, one anchor outcome, and a repair habit. Heroic Monday fantasies are fun — but **boring Wednesday systems** change lives.
