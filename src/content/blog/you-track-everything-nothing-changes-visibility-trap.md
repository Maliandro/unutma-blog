Tracking can become a **comfort ritual**: it feels like control, even when nothing in your life moves.

If you have three apps counting steps, mood, sleep, tasks, and money — and you still feel stuck — you are probably suffering from **visibility without agency**.

## The problem: measurement is not the same as change

Data answers “what happened?” Change requires:

- A **decision**
- A **constraint**
- A **repeatable action**

Tracking without a decision is just **expensive awareness**.

## The fix: one dashboard, one question

Pick **one** metric you will actually use for two weeks — not twenty.

Ask only:

> “What will I do differently **tomorrow** if this number is low?”

If you cannot answer, you are collecting **anxiety souvenirs**.

## Heatmaps and streaks: useful when they serve a question

Visual summaries — like heatmaps — are powerful when they help you see **patterns** (good weeks vs rough weeks) instead of moral scores. They become harmful when you read them as **verdicts** on your worth.

We talk about reading heatmaps without spiraling in [Quick Actions, streaks, and heatmaps](/blog/unutma-quick-actions-streaks-heatmap-statistics/).

## Where Unutma sits

## Bottom line

Tracking everything changes nothing when **nothing is allowed to be ugly**. Choose fewer metrics, connect each to **one** behavior, and let visibility be a **servant** — not a second job.
